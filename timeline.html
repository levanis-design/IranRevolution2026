Chronology

A History of Resistance

Key moments in the Iranian people's enduring struggle for freedom and human dignity.

2025 | The Spark

Economic Collapse and Rising Fury

Protests began peacefully on December 28, 2025, following the closure of Tehran's bazaar due to record currency devaluation and economic despair. Within days, the unrest spread to more than 100 cities nationwide.

January 8-9, 2026 | The Massacres

The Bloodiest Days

Over 48 hours, Iranian security forces carried out organized massacres of protesters. Hospitals were overwhelmed, with at least 217 killed in Tehran alone on January 8. A nationwide internet blackout concealed the true scale.

January 2026 | The Crimson Winter

30,000+ Dead

The crackdown escalated with live ammunition, snipers, heavy weapons, and mass graves. Families were denied truth, mourning, and justice as the death toll continued to rise.

February 2026 | The Reckoning

Confessions and Denials

Officials acknowledged only a fraction of the dead while human rights groups documented far higher numbers. The names gathered here resist that erasure.

Ongoing | The Struggle Continues

We Will Never Forget

The courage of those who stood against tyranny continues to inspire the Iranian diaspora and freedom-loving people worldwide. Their names live on in memory.
